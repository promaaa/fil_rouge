A Simple Magnetic Ipad Stand for a Lazy Night of Netflix

Have you ever been lying in bed, watching something on an iPad propped up on your stomach, and had it slip and come crashing into your nose? This project lets you lie in bed and watch your iPad without that danger. (Of course, use it at your own risk. If the iPad slips out of the bracket, it will still hit you in the face.)

This project came together at the start of COVID. It was made entirely from parts leftover from other projects, which was great for recycling. It has provided hours of movie-watching enjoyment for a couple of years now.

Our bed has a headboard made from tubular steel, so magnets have no problem attaching it. For wooden headboards, we attach steel plates for the magnets to attach to. (Only on IKEA quality furniture. We don't do this to any of the fine woodworking pieces.)

An example of attaching to a headboard made from a different material (e.g., wood) can be seen in our new instructable.

    1. There are a lot of types of DIN rail. The one we use for projects like this is IEC/EN 60715 ‚Äì 35 √ó 15. Specifically ASI PR006-1M Heavy Duty DIN Rail. It is 15mm deep. It is made of steel, not aluminum. This makes it rigid and able to span short distances without torquing.

  4. 4. Step 4: Assembly

    √âtapes :

    1. When assembling the stand, One bracket is locked in between; one is free to slide.
    2. The assembly process
      • Attach the din rail to the magnetic mounts using screws and bolts.
      • Place one of the iPad brackets on the din rail between the two stops on the magnetic mount. This will be the fixed iPad bracket.
      • Place the other iPad bracket between the two magnetic mounts on the din rail. This will be the movable iPad bracket.
      • Place at least four rubberbands in a crisscross pattern between the rubberband mounting post.
      • The next step is not required but highly recommended. Put a piece of gaffers tape or a vinal anti-skid tape over the magnets. Using tape will slightly reduce the magnets' holding power but will help prevent scratching of the surfaces.
      • Attach the magnetic mount to a metal surface.
      • Slide the movable iPad bracket away from the fixed bracket, place an iPad in the groves, and let the brackets slide together. Test to ensure that the Ipad is secure.
      • Test to ensure the magnetic mounts are secure and the iPad is firmly in place.
