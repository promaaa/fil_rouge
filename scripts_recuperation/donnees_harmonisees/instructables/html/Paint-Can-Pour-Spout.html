Paint Can Pour Spout

I have been doing some painting in a bedroom at my house. I wanted to pour paint into a roller tray, but I really dislike the way that the paint spills onto the sides of the can and fills the channel that the lid goes into.

There's an inexpensive product that fits into the channel to make a neat pour spout (the one shown is from Home Depot), and I wanted to make something like that.

  1. 1. Step 1: Cut a Yogurt Container to Make the Spout

    √âtapes :

    1. Using a knife, cut the rim of the container in half, angling the cut through the side of the container so it doesn't remove too much of the bottom of the container. I am leaving all of that plastic so the pour spout will retain its shape. The container's plastic sides are thin, and I'm afraid that they will unexpectedly bend during pouring.
    2. Also cut out a drain hole at the bottom of the container. No real rules about the size. I think that the size shown here turned out to be a bit small. But that all depends on where you intend to pour your paint.
    3. Caution: Be careful not to cut yourself. A knife can take an unexpected turn while cutting the curved plastic.
  2. 2. Step 2: The Rolled Rim of the Container Fits the Paint Lid Groove Exactly

    √âtapes :

    1. Decide in what direction you want the pour spout hole to point. I wanted mine to be placed so the wire handle will support the paint can while the pour spout is centered for a straight pour.
    2. Place the rolled rim of the plastic container into the paint can's groove, then bend the plastic so it fits into the groove. Press down to set the pastic rim into the groove.
    3. In the times I've done this, the plastic rim makes a tight seal in the groove that is pretty stable.
