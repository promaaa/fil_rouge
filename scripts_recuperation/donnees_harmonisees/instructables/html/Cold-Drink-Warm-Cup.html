  1. 1. Step 1: Problem Definition

    √âtapes :

    1. First we need to define our problem. Our goal is to make a warm cup and at the same time keep the drinks cold, so we need a heat insulator as our material. At first, we thought that adding a Thermoelectric module in the cup would be a great idea, but that meant the cup could not be put in a dishwasher. The idea moves on to adding external heat to the cup.
    2. The idea is to make two cups, one for the cold drinks, and one for the warm cup. Then put the cold drink inside the warm cup achieve our goal. Of course, the material is still heat insulator. A material we chose was ceramic, however; two ceramic cups are brittle. So we decided to make the inner cup wood, so it insulate the heat and won't clash with ceramic. The way of adding external heat is by putting the ceramic cup into an oven to heat up the cup.
  2. 2. Step 2: Design

    √âtapes :

    1. The warm cup has to be big enough to hold with two hand, so we measure our diameter with two hands making a circle. This made sure the cup is a good size for holding. We made a model in fusion 360 by using that dimension. Then we made the inner cups, and we realized that the cup wouldn't just fit in through friction(image 1). The solution is to make some locating tabs to support the inner cup to guide to the right place, and this also reduce the heat conduction between the cups(image 2). This creates another problem that there is a gab between two cups(image 3), so we made the top of the inner cup thicker to fill in the gap(image 4).

  4. 4. Step 4: Result

    √âtapes :

    1. The model took about 5 hours to print. The outcome was great, and the tolerance we put in fit perfectly. Then we put the outer cup in an oven to heat up, and put cold milk in the inner cup. The temperature difference was real, but due to the material we used being plastic, the outer cup didn't store a lot of heat. If the cup was made out of ceramic, it would store much more heat in cup and heat up our hands.
