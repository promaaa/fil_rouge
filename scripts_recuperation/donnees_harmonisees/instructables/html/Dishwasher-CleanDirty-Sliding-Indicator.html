Dishwasher Dirty-Clean Sliding Indicator

There are many sliding indicators available on the market. Most of them are made out of plastic or metal. Even if they are very simple they can cost a lot.

Let me present how to build one made out of wood, some glue, paper and a magnet.

  1. 1. Step 1: Cutting Layers - Outer

    √âtapes :

    1. My scrap of a board had a 150mm x 150mm. It allowed me to build a slider 150mm x 50mm.
    2. Cut exactly the same 3 layers of that size (150mm x 50mm). I used a circular saw (it gives a clean cuts) but a handsaw or a knife works as well.
  2. 2. Step 2: Cutting Layers - Inner

    √âtapes :

    1. The slider is built out of 3 layers:
    2. Middle and front layers have cut "windows". A window from the middle layer is bigger (5mm from all of the edges). The front layer is smaller (5mm from the right and left edges and 10mm from the top and bottom edges). Check notes on the first photo.
      • Back layer.
      • Middle layer - it blocks the inner slider to fall out from the sides.
      • Front layer - it blocks the inner slider to fall out in the front.
  3. 3. Step 3: Cutting a Slider

    √âtapes :

    1. Take the middle part you cut out from the middle layer. The height of this part is going to be a desired height of our inner slider. The inner slider needs to cover half of the viewing window. It's enough to just cut the inner part in half.
    2. I recommend to cut it so the width is just a little bit wider than half of the window. This will prevent the situation when the other colour of the 2 parts (Clean - Dirty) is visible when the slider is far right or left.
    3. And believe me - the glue for wood is very strong. I've tried really hard to
    4. A handle needs to be lower than the front window as it has to move smoothly. Use the middle part of the front layer as it already has the height of the front window. Trim it on top and the bottom. You can assemble everything to see if it moves smoothly.
    5. Glue the handle in the middle of the inner slider so it is easy to access it from left and right.
  4. 4. Step 4: Adjusting

    √âtapes :

    1. The slider is designed so it has a small gap between a top part of a middle layer and the back layer. This small gap works as a slot to put a background card inside the slider. Just in case I want to replace it with some other.
    2. Use a file to remove scratches, splinters and uneven edges.
  5. 5. Step 5: Gluing Middle and Back Layers

    √âtapes :

    1. The middle and back layers can be glued together.
  6. 6. Step 6: Painting Slider

    √âtapes :

    1. The slider is going to be touched many-many times. Therefore it needs to be protected. I used an acrylic transparent paint. The inner slider needs to be painted before gluing front and middle layers. Otherwise there is no possibility to paint it on both sides as the inner slider will be locked between these layers.
  7. 7. Step 7: Background

    √âtapes :

    1. As the title says - I used it as an indicator for our dishwasher mashine. Therefore I've printed a Dirty/Clean background, but You can print whatever you want: Open/Close, Occupied/Vacant, etc.
    2. Background's dimensions' are 135mm x 45mm (I've added some margin on top as it is hidden anyway inside the insertion slot).
    3. Slide the background through the insertion slot.
    4. Add the inner slider
  8. 8. Step 8: Gluing Front and Middle Layers

    √âtapes :

    1. When the paint on the inner slider dries out, we can assemble everything and glue the front and middle layers. (Middle layer is already glued to the back layer.)
    2. Remember to insert an inner slider inside the middle layer's window before gluing front and middle layers.
  9. 9. Step 9: Magnet

    √âtapes :

    1. As a magnet I've used a magnet stripe. Other options are to use some neodymium magnet and glue it on the back. Yet another option is to use double-sided tape and stick it directly to the dishwasher's surface. Whatever fits you best.
    2. Please remember to attach some protective layer - e.g. felt - to protect your dishwasher's surface from scratches.
  10. 10. Step 10: Trim

    √âtapes :

    1. As a final step I've trimmed the slider's shape. I've used a file to make corners and edges round.
